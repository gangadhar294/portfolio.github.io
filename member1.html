Maximum Export On Road

Ford-Fulkerson
The Business Case

The industrial area of the city produces a lot of goods every day. But when these goods need to be transported out of the city, the roads that lead out become choked with trucks. Some roads are wide, some are narrow, and some allow only a limited number of vehicles at once. Because of this uneven flow, the total number of goods that can leave the city in a day becomes much lower than what the factories actually produce.

Graph Modeling

Points (Nodes): Important places like gates, junctions, checkpoints. Connections (Edges): Roads connecting these places. Capacity: Every road has a limit—how many trucks can pass per hour. It’s just like pipes carrying water—some pipes are thin, some are wide, and together they decide how much water can reach the end.

Algorithm Analysis

Finding a path where more trucks can still be sent. Sending as many as possible through that path. Repeating this until no more trucks can be sent. At the end, we get the maximum possible truck flow through the city roads.
