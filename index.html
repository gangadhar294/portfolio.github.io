Concept 01

Algorithmic Efficiency

Algorithm efficiency measures how effectively an algorithm utilizes computational resources, primarily time and memory, to solve a problem. It is a critical concept in computer science, as it determines an algorithm's practical applicability, especially when dealing with large datasets or complex computations.

Ex: Merge Sort , Bubble Sort , FFT

Concept 02

Time & Space Complexity

Measures used to describe the efficiency of an algorithm, indicating how its resource requirements (time and memory, respectively) scale as the size of the input data increases. This analysis is generally expressed using Big O notation, which provides a standardized, machine-independent way to compare algorithms, focusing on their worst-case performance for large inputs.

Ex: O(1) [Hash Map] , O(N log N) [Quick Sort] , O(N¬≤) [Bubble Sort]
